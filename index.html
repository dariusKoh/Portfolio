Competitions

I have listed most of the competitions, Hackathons, and Festivals that I participated in.

Perse Coding Team Challenge 2022

The Perse Coding Team Challenge is a competition where students submit short code solutions to various challenges that increase in difficulty across two timed rounds. Only those who scored a Distinction in round 1 can proceed to the next round. In Round 1, as we can only work with one computer, we must complete as many questions as possible in 40 minutes. As we proceeded to Round 2, the questions were much more challenging, although we could use our computers. I learnt the importance of communication when coding in a team and how to solve problems without a compiler. https://persecoding.net/

Tech For Good 2021

Tech For Good (T4G) is an annual innovation festival by Engineering Good. Its objective is to use technology to help people with disabilities and the elderly live a life of opportunities. My group (Team DHS) decided to create an Accessible Control System for Gaming for the Muscular Dystrophy Association. Using an Arduino, we created an environmentally and budget-friendly product. It is intended to be customizable so users can easily modify them for their computer or console games. We continued to develop the project in our Secondary 4 Computing+ Coursework. More information on the Accessible Control System can be found under "Projects". https://engineeringgood.org/assistive-tech/tech-for-good-2021/ https://engineeringgood.org/team/dhs/

Australian Informatics Olympiad 2021

The AIO is a 3-hour computer programming competition where students invent algorithms and write code to solve problems. I joined this competition to test my knowledge. I learnt how to make my code more efficient and thus developed more confidence programming in a stressful environment. In the end, I achieved a bronze award. https://www.amt.edu.au/aio

HackWise 2020

HackWise 2020 is a hackathon where groups were to develop solutions to "make a world a better place" in 3 days. My group's vision was to create an application that detects the user's incorrect posture through a computer's webcam. We train the neural network using machine learning to differentiate poor posture from good ones. It is the first time we have used a learning machine model to create an application. https://devpost.com/software/posturecorrect

Projects

Some of the projects I have created are listed here. These include personal projects, projects completed from courses or school projects.

Adaptive Gaming Controller

The Adaptive Gaming Controller was made in partnership with the Muscular Dystrophy Association (Singapore) and Engineering Good during Tech For Good 2021. The project was then later continued during our computing coursework in 2022. Believing that everyone should be able to enjoy gaming, my team designed and developed a customizable, affordable and accessible controller for everyone to use. Through this experience, I learned how technology could improve and impacts the world around us and the process of developing a prototype and a business case for such a product. Click here for more information on our project. https://engineeringgood.org/team/dhs/ https://github.com/Shanjiith-Pranov/2022-Computing-Coursework

MacRitchie Trails

My group created an IOS app called MacRitchie Trails during the Swift Accelerator Programme 2020. The app shows the map of MacRitchie reservoir, with information on landmarks, locations of restrooms, shelters and more, aiming to help hikers to explore the area. While deepening my skillsets in IOS app creation, I have also attempted new and different tools such as MapKit in this project. Despite many failures, I keep trying and learning new tools and skillsets, honing my resilience. https://apps.apple.com/us/app/macritchie-trails/id1547956127
